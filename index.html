    When Mining Healthcare Data Meets AI Ethics:
    Towards Privacy-preservation, Fairness and Trustworthy

    Data mining techniques have been successfully implemented in many different domains to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the workflows. Mining healthcare data offers profound new opportunities to improve clinical diagnosis, personalised medication, and treatment recommendation; however, the performance of currently state-the-arts techniques are primarily relied on quality, diverse, annotated data to be effective. Such kind of data is difficult to obtain in the healthcare domain, given the non-biases nature, geographical significance, and confidentiality requirements in AI ethical constrains. In this talk, we will discuss how utilising the federated learning framework, weakly supervised techniques and few-shots learning approaches can address the above challenges and improve the perspective of trustworthy, fairness, and privacy-preserving of techniques. In the end, we will demonstrate the potential of next generation mining techniques that offer privacy protection and therefore have broad implications for the future of digital health and healthcare informatics.

    Target Audience

    The proposed tutorial targets a broad range of audience, including students, researchers and healthcare practitioners who want to catch up with the problems, algorithms, and applications in the area of mining healthcare data. Besides, we also welcome any participant with related knowledge on data mining to join the tutorial. Since healthcare data bears many properties that may be shared by data from other application domains, solutions to real-world healthcare data may also benefit other applications.

    Although we will introduce the basics of knowledge required, it is better to have some knowledge in college-level mathematics and data mining. The emphasis will be on introducing the special challenges brought by healthcare applications, and the intuition on methodologies to solve those challenges. The tutorial will be self-contained in a high level.
